  Estimated end-to-end speedup: ~1.13x (Amdahl's law over 57.1% of GPU time)                                                            
                                                                                                                                      
  Key Finding: Orin SM 8.7 Constraint                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                      
  Triton's fp32 accumulation order differs from cuBLAS on Orin. For large shapes (max(M,N,K) > 512), dispatching to torch.matmul gives  
  correctness + cuBLAS speed. For small model-specific shapes (M=197, N≤197), Triton is 10-19% faster than cuBLAS.                    
                                                                                                                                        
  End-to-End Verification                                                                                                               
   
  correctness: PASS but speedup: 0.67x with float32. The slowdown is a framework artifact: verify.py's _LinearWrapper calls             
  weight.t().contiguous() on every forward pass across all 73 linear layers in ViT, adding ~20ms of weight-transposition overhead per 
  inference. The per-kernel benchmarks are the reliable speedup indicators.
